# Session Transcript: Stage 008 — Bivariate Report and Combined Synthesis

## Session Overview

Stage 008 produced two documents synthesising the bivariate GP results from
Stage 007: a second research memo covering the σ-extension findings, and a
standalone final synthesis combining both analyses. The session involved no
technical implementation — only writing and framing decisions.

## Design Decisions Made

### Decision 1: dw_obs as new finding, not correction of Stage 002/003
**Chosen:** The dw_obs dominance finding is framed as specific to the σ-active
bivariate analysis, not as a parameter previously missed.
**Rationale:** Stage 002/003 used a different parameter set; dw_obs gating the
observational channel is only meaningful when σ is active. No prior gap.
**Tradeoffs:** Requires a brief explanation of why the parameter sets differ.
**Proposed by:** agent

### Decision 2: Non-amplification as midpoint-configuration result
**Chosen:** Ψ ≤ 0.714 throughout the bivariate phase is described as the
analysis not re-entering the amplification regime rather than σ suppressing it.
**Rationale:** The phase slices fix lambda at midpoints; Stage 002's amplification
required small lambda. The bivariate analysis simply didn't sample that corner.
**Tradeoffs:** More qualified language; the distinction must be explicit.
**Proposed by:** agent

### Decision 3: No references section
**Chosen:** Both Stage 008 documents omit the references section present in the
Stage 004 report. Plain `.md` only; no `.myst` or `.bib`.
**Rationale:** User instruction.
**Proposed by:** mpadge

### Decision 4: Final report as standalone synthesis
**Chosen:** `docs/final-report.md` restates all three findings from scratch
rather than assuming the reader has read the prior reports.
**Rationale:** A synthesis that presupposes prior reading is only useful to
readers who have already read them — the opposite of a synthesis document's purpose.
**Tradeoffs:** Minor duplication with `docs/004-first-report/report.md`.
**Proposed by:** joint

### Decision 5: Shared docs/figures/ for final report
**Chosen:** Final report figures go in `docs/figures/` alongside both Stage 004
and Stage 007 figures copied there.
**Rationale:** Final report is in `docs/` directly; sibling `figures/` is the
natural convention for relative path references.
**Proposed by:** agent

## Tradeoffs Considered

- **Addendum vs. standalone for final report**: standalone wins — a synthesis
  that requires the prior reports has no independent value.
- **Claim σ suppresses amplification vs. midpoint configuration**: midpoint
  framing wins — stronger claim is not supported by the evidence.
